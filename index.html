6A: Verb + -ing and infinitive with to (*without to)

➤ Gerund (verb + -ing)

After prepositions and phrasal verbs

Nota: Recuerda que después de CUALQUIER preposición (in, at, on, of, for, about, etc.), el verbo siempre debe ir en -ing. "In" es la preposición y "-ing" es el verbo.

  • • I apologize for interrupting.
  • • She asked about making another cup of tea.
  • • We talked about going to Italy for our summer vacations.

The subject of a sentence (actions)

  • • Shopping is my favourite thing to do at weekends.
  • • Reading books it's the best way to learn.
  • • I like drinking coffee at breakfast.

After specific verbs: like, dislike, love, hate, prefer and enjoy

  • • I hate washing dishes.
  • • I love listening rock music.

Other common verbs

admit, avoid, consider, deny, feel like, finish, imagine, keep, mind, miss, practise, recommend, risk, spend time, stop, suggest.

  • • She avoided talking about the problem.
  • • He suggested meeting later.

After set phrases

can (not) help, be (not) worth, there is no point (Nota: 'There is no point' suele llevar 'in' implícito o explícito, lo que nos lleva a la regla de las preposiciones + -ing).

➤ The infinitive with to

After verbs that express intention, decision or promise

agree, decide, expect, hope, learn, need, plan, promise, refuse, want, would like.

  • • She decided to go abroad.
  • • I hope to see you soon.
  • • They refused to pay the bill.

After verbs

afford, ask, forget, help, offer, wish, choose, manage, prepare, pretend, remember, seem, wait.

  • • I asked him to show me the book.
  • • She helped to put away the dishes.

After it + adjectives

  • • It was nice to see you again Paul.
  • • It is important to learn the prepositions.

To express a reason or purpose (why)

  • • She saves money to buy a car.
  • • I tried not to talk about politics to avoid arguing.

BE CAREFUL: Tricky Verbs

Verbs like: forget, stop, remember, go, try and regret can be followed by -ing or infinitive with to but the meaning changes.

Forget

+ gerund = like a memory / + infinitive = forget something that needs to be done

  • • I will never forget going to the beach with my family when I was 10 years old.
  • • I forgot to buy milk at the store.

Remember

+ gerund = (like a memory) / + infinitive = remember something that needs to be done

  • • She remembers watching this TV show when she was young.
  • • I remembered to bring the cake.

Regret

+ gerund = regret a past action / + infinitive = feel sorry to tell somebody about something (formal)

  • • I regret eating too much last night.
  • • We regret to inform you that your application has been denied.

Stop

+ gerund = stop a continuous action or habit / + infinitive = stop something to do another action

  • • She stopped smoking.
  • • She stopped to eat.

Go

+ gerund = recreative activities/sports / + infinitive = movement with purpose

  • • I went swimming.
  • • I went to buy bread.

➤ The infinitive without to

After modal verbs

can, could, may, might, will, shall, would, should, must, but NOT ought to and have to.

After some verbs of perception

see, watch, hear, notice, feel, sense.

  • • I watched a film.
  • • If you feel ill, go to the doctor's.
  • • I saw you walking down the street.
  • • My dog heard a noise and started barking.

After make, let and help

  • • You made me cry.
  • • Let me help you with the heavy bags.

After expressions with 'why'

  • • Why you go clubbing the night before an exam?
