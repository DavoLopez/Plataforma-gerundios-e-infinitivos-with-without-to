6A: Verb + -ing and infinitive with to (*without to)

6A: Verbos seguidos de -ing e infinitivo con (o sin) 'to'

Conceptos fundamentales:

Gerundio (-ing): En inglés, usamos el gerundio para tratar una acción como si fuera un sustantivo (una cosa o un concepto). Si la acción es el sujeto o va después de una preposición, necesitamos el -ing.

Infinitivo (to + verbo): Suele expresar dirección, propósito, intención o futuro. El "to" funciona como una flecha que apunta hacia adelante.

➤ Gerund (verb + -ing)

➤ Gerundio (verbo + -ing)

After prepositions and phrasal verbs

Después de preposiciones y phrasal verbs. Regla de Oro: Después de CUALQUIER preposición (in, at, on, of, for, about...), el verbo siempre va en -ing.

  • • I apologize for interrupting.
  • • She asked about making another cup of tea.
  • • We talked about going to Italy for our summer vacations.

The subject of a sentence (actions)

Como sujeto de una oración (acciones).

  • • Shopping is my favourite thing to do at weekends.
  • • Reading books it's the best way to learn.
  • • I like drinking coffee at breakfast.

After specific verbs: like, dislike, love, hate, prefer and enjoy

Después de verbos específicos que denotan gusto o preferencia.

  • • I hate washing dishes.
  • • I love listening rock music.

Other common verbs

Otros verbos comunes (admit, avoid, consider, deny, feel like, finish, imagine, keep, mind, miss, practise, recommend, risk, spend time, stop, suggest).

  • • She avoided talking about the problem.
  • • He suggested meeting later.

After set phrases

Después de expresiones fijas (can (not) help, be (not) worth, there is no point). Nota: 'There is no point' suele llevar 'in' implícito o explícito, lo que nos lleva a la regla de las preposiciones + -ing.

➤ The infinitive with to

➤ El infinitivo con 'to'

After verbs that express intention, decision or promise

Después de verbos que expresan intención, decisión o promesa (agree, decide, expect, hope, learn, need, plan, promise, refuse, want, would like).

  • • She decided to go abroad.
  • • I hope to see you soon.
  • • They refused to pay the bill.

After verbs

Después de otros verbos (afford, ask, forget, help, offer, wish, choose, manage, prepare, pretend, remember, seem, wait).

  • • I asked him to show me the book.
  • • She helped to put away the dishes.

After it + adjectives

Después de "it" + adjetivos.

  • • It was nice to see you again Paul.
  • • It is important to learn the prepositions.

To express a reason or purpose (why)

Para expresar una razón o propósito (¿por qué?).

  • • She saves money to buy a car.
  • • I tried not to talk about politics to avoid arguing.

BE CAREFUL: Tricky Verbs

¡Cuidado! Verbos capciosos (el significado cambia según la forma)

Forget

+ gerund = como un recuerdo / + infinitive = olvidar algo que debe hacerse

  • • I will never forget going to the beach with my family when I was 10 years old.
  • • I forgot to buy milk at the store.

Remember

+ gerund = (como un recuerdo) / + infinitive = recordar algo que debe hacerse

  • • She remembers watching this TV show when she was young.
  • • I remembered to bring the cake.

Regret

+ gerund = arrepentirse de una acción pasada / + infinitive = sentir decir algo (lenguaje formal)

  • • I regret eating too much last night.
  • • We regret to inform you that your application has been denied.

Stop

+ gerund = detener una acción o hábito continuo / + infinitive = detener algo para hacer otra acción

  • • She stopped smoking.
  • • She stopped to eat.

Go

+ gerund = actividades recreativas o deportes / + infinitive = desplazamiento con un propósito

  • • I went swimming.
  • • I went to buy bread.

➤ The infinitive without to

➤ El infinitivo sin 'to'

After modal verbs

can, could, may, might, will, shall, would, should, must, but NOT ought to and have to.

After some verbs of perception

see, watch, hear, notice, feel, sense.

  • • I watched a film.
  • • If you feel ill, go to the doctor's.
  • • I saw you walking down the street.
  • • My dog heard a noise and started barking.

After make, let and help

  • • You made me cry.
  • • Let me help you with the heavy bags.

After expressions with 'why'

  • • Why you go clubbing the night before an exam?
